Jacob A. Riis and the Tenements of New York City

Born 1849 in Denmark, Riis emigrated to the United States in 1870. He initially worked as a carpenter, salesman, and writer with minimal success.

Later, he became the editor of several small newspapers and used the newly discovered technique of flash photography to document conditions of the city's poorest areas. Scribner's Magazine published an article featuring his photography in 1889. Due to demand and interest, this was expanded to form the book we know today, which was first published by Charles Scribner's Sons in 1890.
